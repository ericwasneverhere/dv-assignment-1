Subtheme: [Substance Overdoses]

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How has alcohol’s role in overdose deaths changed over time?
    Alcohol has long been one of the most widely used and socially accepted substances, but its role in fatal overdoses may be shifting. I read somewhere that younger generations are drinking less than previous ones, while more lethal substances like fentanyl dominate the current overdose crisis. Exploring alcohol’s involvement over time can reveal whether its contribution to overdose deaths has truly declined, and how its role compares to the rise of other drugs.
  2. How do drug involvement patterns vary by race?
    Different communities can be affected by the overdose crisis in different ways. Some drugs show up more often in certain groups than others, and looking at these patterns can help explain why the crisis doesn’t look the same for everyone.
  3. How do urban vs suburban zip codes differ?
    Where someone lives can change the risks they face. City neighborhoods and suburban areas often have different access to drugs, healthcare, and emergency response, which might show up in the overdose data.

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Fentanyl has completely reshaped the overdose crisis, pushing older drivers like heroin and prescription opioids to the side. Alcohol is still present in a steady way, but it plays more of a supporting role, usually alongside other drugs. Different groups are affected in different ways — Black residents see more cocaine involvement, while White residents see more alcohol and prescription drugs. Urban areas face the biggest overdose numbers overall, while suburban areas have smaller but differently balanced patterns.
